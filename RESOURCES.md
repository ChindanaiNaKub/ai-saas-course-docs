# AI-era SaaS Resources

## Knowledge

- [YC Startup Library: "Do Things That Don't Scale"](https://www.ycombinator.com/library/96-do-things-that-don-t-scale)
  Use for: early-stage founder behavior, especially manually recruiting users and learning before scaling.
- [YC Startup Library: "YC's Essential Startup Advice"](https://www.ycombinator.com/library/4D-yc-s-essential-startup-advice)
  Use for: the compact startup basics: launch, talk to users, build what people want, and keep momentum.
- [YC Startup Library: "How to Plan an MVP"](https://www.ycombinator.com/library/6f-how-to-plan-an-mvp)
  Use for: deciding the smallest useful thing to build after enough customer evidence exists.
- [YC Startup Library: "How to Build an MVP"](https://www.ycombinator.com/library/Io-how-to-build-an-mvp)
  Use for: keeping MVP scope narrow and focused on learning from first users.
- [YC Startup Library: "The Sales Playbook for Founders"](https://www.ycombinator.com/library/Mo-the-sales-playbook-for-founders)
  Use for: founder-led sales process, running tighter calls, and closing early customers.
- [YC Startup Library: "Enterprise Sales for Founders"](https://www.ycombinator.com/library/LF-enterprise-sales-for-founders)
  Use for: understanding sales steps, qualification, and closing first B2B customers when deals are larger or more complex.
- [YC Startup Library: "How to Get Your First Customers"](https://www.ycombinator.com/library/Ip-how-to-get-your-first-customers)
  Use for: tactical early customer acquisition and founder-led customer development.
- [First Round Review: "Founder-Led Growth Playbook"](https://review.firstround.com/founder-led-growth-playbook/)
  Use for: tactical founder-led growth and learning directly from customer-facing work.
- [Lean Startup Co.: "What Is an MVP? Eric Ries Explains"](https://leanstartup.co/resources/articles/what-is-an-mvp/)
  Use for: the core definition of MVP as maximum validated learning with the least effort.
- [Steve Blank: "A Path to the Minimum Viable Product"](https://steveblank.com/2021/04/20/the-secret-to-the-minimum-viable-product/)
  Use for: linking MVP design to customer archetypes, jobs to be done, and execution.
- [Brian Balfour: "Product Channel Fit Will Make or Break Your Growth Strategy"](https://brianbalfour.com/essays/product-channel-fit-for-growth)
  Use for: understanding why product and distribution cannot be designed separately.
- [Brian Balfour: "Four Fits For $100M+ Growth"](https://brianbalfour.com/four-fits-growth-framework)
  Use for: seeing product, market, channel, and model as one system rather than isolated decisions.
- [April Dunford: "A Quickstart Guide to Positioning"](https://www.aprildunford.com/post/a-quickstart-guide-to-positioning)
  Use for: positioning as leadership in something a well-defined customer segment cares about.
- [April Dunford: "Startup Positioning: A Template"](https://medium.com/@aprildunford/startup-positioning-a-template-c15b010827de)
  Use for: turning evidence into a concise positioning statement for early-stage products.
- [Christensen Institute: "Jobs to Be Done Theory"](https://www.christenseninstitute.org/theory/jobs-to-be-done/)
  Use for: understanding why customers choose products based on the circumstances and progress they seek.
- [Wynter: "B2B Message Layers Framework"](https://wynter.com/post/b2b-message-layers-framework-wynter)
  Use for: checking whether B2B messaging answers what the product is, who it is for, why it matters, and why choose it.
- [The Mom Test by Rob Fitzpatrick](https://www.momtestbook.com/)
  Use for: customer conversations that reveal real behavior instead of polite encouragement.
- [Sachin Rekhi: "A Primer on Talking to Customers From Rob Fitzpatrick's The Mom Test"](https://www.sachinrekhi.com/p/the-mom-test-rob-fitzpatrick)
  Use for: quick review of asking about the customer's life, past behavior, and concrete problems.
- [DataReportal: "Digital 2026: Thailand"](https://datareportal.com/reports/digital-2026-thailand)
  Use for: current Thailand digital context, including internet penetration, mobile connections, and social media usage.
- [Stripe: "SaaS Metrics: A Complete Guide to Tracking Business Growth"](https://stripe.com/resources/more/essential-saas-metrics)
  Use for: understanding acquisition, engagement, retention, growth, and unit economics once a product has paying users.
- [Bain: "Per-Seat Software Pricing Isn't Dead, but New Models Are Gaining Steam"](https://www.bain.com/insights/per-seat-software-pricing-isnt-dead-but-new-models-are-gaining-steam/)
  Use for: AI-era SaaS pricing shifts, especially hybrid, usage-based, and outcome-based pricing.
- [a16z: "AI Is Driving A Shift Towards Outcome-Based Pricing"](https://a16z.com/newsletter/december-2024-enterprise-newsletter-ai-is-driving-a-shift-towards-outcome-based-pricing/)
  Use for: thinking about pricing AI products by value delivered rather than only seats.
- [OpenAI: "How your data is used to improve model performance"](https://help.openai.com/en/articles/5722486-how-your-data-is-used-to-improve-model-performance)
  Use for: explaining API/business data-use defaults and how to discuss model training concerns with customers.
- [OpenAI: "Enterprise privacy at OpenAI"](https://openai.com/enterprise-privacy/)
  Use for: checking OpenAI business data privacy claims when building AI products on OpenAI services.
- [OWASP: "Top 10 for Large Language Model Applications"](https://owasp.org/www-project-top-10-for-large-language-model-applications/)
  Use for: AI app security risks such as prompt injection, sensitive information disclosure, and insecure output handling.
- [EDPS: "Data minimization"](https://www.edps.europa.eu/data-protection/data-protection/glossary/d_en)
  Use for: the principle of collecting only data directly relevant and necessary for the purpose.
- [GDPR-info: "Article 5 GDPR: Principles relating to processing of personal data"](https://gdpr-info.eu/art-5-gdpr/)
  Use for: privacy principles such as lawfulness, purpose limitation, data minimization, accuracy, storage limitation, integrity, and confidentiality.
- [DLA Piper Data Protection Laws of the World: Thailand](https://www.dlapiperdataprotection.com/index.html?c=TH&t=law)
  Use for: high-level Thai PDPA overview, including controllers, processors, lawful bases, and data subject rights.

## Wisdom (Communities)

- [Indie Hackers](https://www.indiehackers.com/)
  Use for: reading real indie founder journeys, revenue numbers, launch attempts, and validation stories.
- [MicroConf Connect](https://microconf.com/connect)
  Use for: bootstrapped SaaS peer advice; best when the user wants a serious founder community rather than generic startup noise.
- [Bangkok Startup Association](https://bkkstartup.com/)
  Use for: local founder network, events, and Thailand-specific context.
- [Meetup: Bangkok Startup Founder 101](https://www.meetup.com/bangkok-startup-founder-101/)
  Use for: finding in-person conversations with founders and operators in Bangkok.
- [Facebook: Indie Hackers Chiang Mai](https://www.facebook.com/groups/indiehackerschiangmai/)
  Use for: Thailand-based indie hacker connection, especially for builders outside Bangkok or remote-friendly circles.
