# Mission: AI-era SaaS that real customers will buy

## Why
The user wants to stop repeating the pattern of spending 2-3 months building a web product that nobody uses. They want to learn how SaaS still wins in an AI era where building is easy, and how to validate demand and distribution before overbuilding, especially from the perspective of a Thai founder selling to markets with different willingness to pay.

## Success looks like
- Identify whether an idea has a reachable customer segment before building the product.
- Design a small validation test that proves pain, budget, urgency, and a distribution path.
- Choose between Thailand-first, global-first, or niche B2B markets with clear reasoning.
- Build smaller prototypes only after evidence from customer conversations, pre-sales, pilots, or repeated channel signals.

## Constraints
- Avoid spending months coding before evidence of demand.
- The user is Thai and is concerned that Thai buyers may be more price-sensitive than US or European buyers.
- Lessons should be practical, direct, and grounded in real founder behavior, not abstract startup theory.

## Out of scope
- Venture-scale fundraising strategy.
- Large-company enterprise sales playbooks before the user has a validated niche.
- Building full products before distribution and willingness-to-pay evidence.
